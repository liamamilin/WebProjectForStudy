Dr. Norman Borlaug: Father of the Green Revolution

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world food security. Through his work in agricultural science, he developed drought-resistant and high-yielding varieties of wheat, which revolutionized food production in developing countries.

His work saved hundreds of millions of lives and laid the groundwork for future advancements in agriculture and global food policy.
